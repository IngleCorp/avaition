Computer Number

Computer number is required to each candidate in order to appear for the airmen examination in DGCA and to obtain and Indian DGCA license.computer number is simply a way to create a file with all your details which are to be approved by DGCA for a individual record of a candidate.

What is Computer Number ?

Computer Number is your unique identity available with Central Examination Organisation (CEO), DGCA for appearing in all Pilot examinations. Candidates must apply for Computer Number as soon as they receive the mark sheet / Pass certificate, plus verifications of these certificates from their respective education boards.It will be desirable for you to have your Computer Number allotted by DGCA before joining the school, as this would facilitate future processes with relative ease. Once a candidate registers with us for Ground Classes of all the subjects, we provide complete assistance in applying and for the issuance of DGCA computer number.

How to obtain computer number ?

Logo on to Pariksha

Visit https://pariksha.dgca.gov.in Press 'New Candidate Registration' and read 'INSTRUCTIONS TO THE CANDIDATES-FLIGHT CREW'

Filling the form

Fill up the form with your personal details as per your documents along with a valid email ID and mobile number. Finish filling up the form followed by entering the OTP received on your mobile number.

Password generation

Sign in to your registered email ID to see your temperory computer number and follow the link from pariksha to generate your password.

Candidate login

Visit https://pariksha.dgca.gov.in/home and select 'Candidate Login'. Sign in with your credentials. Enter your address, fill up your educational qualification and upload the supporting documents. Finally review the details and documents and the submit the application.

Submitting the hard copy

Login to your email ID to find your application form. Take a print out and attach your picture to it and send it to the following address within 10days of submission.

DGCA Subjects

Syllubus

  • Air navigation

    The basic principles of air navigation are identical to general navigation, which includes the process of planning, recording, and controlling the movement of a craft from one place to another. Air navigation, including the use of aeronautical charts, instruments and navigation aids; an understanding of the principles and characteristics of appropriate navigation systems; operation of airborne equipment; practical aspects of air navigation and dead reckoning techniques.

    1. Platform - Online
    2. Total questions - 100
    3. Total Marks - 100
    4. Minimum Passing mark - 70

  • Air regulation

    The Directorate General of Civil Aviation is the regulatory body in the field of Civil Aviation primarily dealing with safety issues. It is responsible for regulation of air transport services to/from/within India and for enforcement of civil air regulations, air safety and airworthiness standards. It also co-ordinates all regulatory functions with International Civil Aviation Organisation. This particular subjects gives you a deeper understanding about DGCA and ICAO along with the laws pertaining and applicable to airmen.

    1. Exam Platform - Online
    2. Total questions - 50
    3. Total Marks - 100
    4. Minimum Passing mark - 70

  • Air metrology

    Aviation meteorology is the study of weather from the unique perspective of the aviation industry. This subject began during the era of hot-air balloons and gliders. Study of aviation meteorology is rewarding: in terms both of saving lives and of reducing injuries and as a scientific pursuit in its own right. An Interpretation and application of aeronautical meteorological reports, charts and forecasts, use of, and procedures for obtaining, meteorological information, pre-flight and in-flight; altimetry.

    1. Exam Platform - Online
    2. Total questions - 50
    3. Total Marks - 100
    4. Minimum Passing mark - 70

  • Air technicals

    Deals with the basics principles of flight, aerodynamics and a detailed overview about aircraft engines and systems

    1. Exam Platform - Offline
    2. Total questions - 100
    3. Total Marks - 100
    4. Minimum Passing mark - 70
