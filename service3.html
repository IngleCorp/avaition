DGCA CPL Conversion

Process

Covertion of a foreign CPL to Indian CPL is basically simplified to three different phases. There are alternate ways in which covertion can be done, however the below mentioned is the most preferred method.

Phase-I BEFORE GOING ABROAD

  1. Minimum 17 years of age
  2. Class II medical certificate
  3. Candidate should have completed 10th board exam (or equivalent) from a recognized board Examination

After applying for PPL, candidates must clear two examinations by DGCA (PPL Composite, PPL Technical). Flying Requirements Candidate should have at least 40 hours of flying time (as pilot) under his/her belt as per DGCA syllabus. Flying training can be obtained from a reputed and recognized flying school.


Phase-II ABROAD

  1. Minimum 17 years of age
  2. Class II medical certificate
  3. Candidate should have completed 10th board exam (or equivalent) from a recognized board Examination

After applying for PPL, candidates must clear two examinations by DGCA (PPL Composite, PPL Technical). Flying Requirements Candidate should have at least 40 hours of flying time (as pilot) under his/her belt as per DGCA syllabus. Flying training can be obtained from a reputed and recognized flying school.

AFTER COMING BACK TO INDIA

  1. Minimum 17 years of age
  2. Class II medical certificate
  3. Candidate should have completed 10th board exam (or equivalent) from a recognized board Examination

• Flying Skill Tests
Before flying in India, the trainee has to ensure that he has the following licenses (Usually processed by the flying schools in India)
a) SPL (Student Pilot License) - Issued based on the DGCA papers cleared by the trainee
b) FRTOL (Flight Radio Telephone Operator's License) - Issued based on RTR
After meeting the mentioned prequsites, trainee has to give the following skill tests in India a) GFT Day
b) GFT Night
c) 250 nm cross-country test by day
d) 120 nm cross-country test by night
e) IR Test (If IR endorsed)
 • Signal/Morse code test ( could be done either in India or Abroad)
